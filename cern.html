Project Deep Dive

My research focuses on the performance optimisation for the tH(bb) process, a rare interaction where a Higgs boson is produced alongside a single top quark. The primary challenge is to isolate the faint signal of this event from immense background noise generated by other particle interactions.

  • Advanced Model Optimization: I led the development and fine-tuning of an XGBoost model for signal-background classification. This involved extensive hyperparameter optimization and a comparative analysis against other architectures, including Neural Networks, to push for the highest possible performance.
  • Statistical Analysis & Validation: I utilized the TRExFitter framework to perform rigorous statistical analysis of the model's output. This included incorporating systematic uncertainties to ensure the results were robust and accurately reflected real-world experimental conditions.
  • Maximizing Discovery Potential: The core objective was to maximize the "significance" metric—a key statistical measure in particle physics. The final model demonstrated a consistently high significance, improving upon previous benchmarks and increasing the potential for precise measurements of the Higgs boson.
  • Data Pipeline Management: I worked with large-scale datasets (petabytes) using the ROOT framework, developing analysis workflows in C++ and Python to preprocess data, evaluate models, and prepare results for final statistical fitting.

Highlight: Conference Presentation

I had the privilege of presenting our latest analysis results on top-Higgs boson production at the Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft (DPG) meeting in Göttingen, Germany.

This was an incredible opportunity to share my work with the wider physics community, engage in discussions with leading researchers, and represent the contributions of our team on an international stage.
